LEARNLN_01concrete
What Is Division?
Division means sharing equally or making equal groups. 18 ÷ 6 means: put 18 into 6 equal groups — how many in each group?
🚩 mark
LEARNLN_02concrete
Model with Counters
To solve 18 ÷ 6: draw 6 circles. Place 1 counter in each, then repeat. When all 18 counters are used, count: 3 in each circle. So 18 ÷ 6 = 3.
🚩 mark
LEARNLN_03pictorial
Connect to Multiplication
Think: 6 × ? = 18. Since 6 × 3 = 18, then 18 ÷ 6 = 3. Division and multiplication are related — one undoes the other.
🚩 mark
LEARNLN_04pictorial
Bar Model for Division
A bar model shows division visually. For 24 ÷ 4: draw a bar labeled 24. Divide it into 4 equal parts. Each part = 24 ÷ 4 = 6. The bar model shows the whole (24) and the parts (6 each).
🚩 mark
LEARNLN_05abstract
Parts of a Division Equation
In 18 ÷ 6 = 3: the dividend (18) is the total being divided; the divisor (6) is how many groups; the quotient (3) is how many in each group. Always ask: total ÷ groups = each.

LEARNLN_01concrete
Finding the Size of Each Group
When you know the total and the number of groups, divide to find how many in each group. 28 ÷ 7 = 4 means 28 items in 7 groups = 4 in each.
🚩 mark
LEARNLN_02concrete
Using Counters
Put out 7 circles. Deal 28 counters one at a time into each circle. Keep going until all 28 are placed. Count how many in each circle: 4. So 28 ÷ 7 = 4.
🚩 mark
LEARNLN_03pictorial
Check with Multiplication
If 28 ÷ 7 = 4, check: 7 × 4 = 28. ✓ Always verify: groups × size = total.
🚩 mark
LEARNLN_04pictorial
Bar Model for Group Size
Draw a bar for the total (28). Mark 7 equal parts — one for each group. The unknown size is 28 ÷ 7 = 4 in each part. Bar models help you see: total ÷ groups = each.
🚩 mark
LEARNLN_05abstract
The Division Equation
When you know the total and number of groups, write: Total ÷ Groups = Each. Example: 28 total ÷ 7 groups = 4 each. The answer (quotient) tells you the size of each group. Check: 7 × 4 = 28. ✓

🚩 mark
LEARNLN_01concrete
Finding the Number of Groups
When you know the total and how many in each group, divide to find the number of groups. 24 ÷ 8 = 3 means 24 items, 8 in each group, makes 3 groups.
🚩 mark
LEARNLN_02concrete
Model It with Circles
For 35 ÷ 7: circle groups of 7 from 35 counters. First circle: 7. Second: 14. Third: 21. Fourth: 28. Fifth: 35. You make 5 circles. So 35 ÷ 7 = 5 groups.
🚩 mark
LEARNLN_03pictorial
Skip Count Up
To find 36 ÷ 9: count by 9s → 9, 18, 27, 36. That's 4 counts. So 36 ÷ 9 = 4 groups. Skip counting shows you how many equal jumps fit into the total.
🚩 mark
LEARNLN_04pictorial
Number Line: Counting Groups
Use a number line to find 24 ÷ 6. Start at 0, jump 6 each time: 0 → 6 → 12 → 18 → 24. Count the jumps: 4. So 24 ÷ 6 = 4 groups. Each jump is one group of 6.
🚩 mark
LEARNLN_05abstract
The Division Equation
When you know the total and size of each group, write: Total ÷ Each = Groups. Example: 35 total ÷ 7 each = 5 groups. Compare to finding group size: Total ÷ Groups = Each. Both use division — only the unknown changes.

LEARNLN_01concrete
What Is a Bar Model?
A bar model is a rectangle that represents the total. You divide it into equal sections to show division. The total goes on top, the equal parts go below. Each part shows the value of one group.
🚩 mark
LEARNLN_02concrete
Drawing a Bar Model
To solve 24 ÷ 4: Step 1 — draw a long bar labeled 24. Step 2 — split it into 4 equal parts. Step 3 — write ? in each part. Step 4 — compute: 24 ÷ 4 = 6. Write 6 in each part.
🚩 mark
LEARNLN_03pictorial
Two Types of Division
Bar models show both types: (1) 'How many in each group?' — you know the number of groups. Draw that many parts, find each part's value. (2) 'How many groups?' — you know each part's size. Count how many parts fit.
🚩 mark
LEARNLN_04pictorial
Reading a Bar Model
A bar model has three key parts: (1) The total (the whole bar). (2) The number of equal parts (the divisor). (3) The value of each part (the quotient). If the bar shows 42 split into 7 parts, the equation is 42 ÷ 7 = 6.
🚩 mark
LEARNLN_05abstract
Bar Model and the Equation
Every bar model maps directly to a division equation: Total ÷ Parts = Each. Example: bar labeled 56, split into 7 parts → 56 ÷ 7 = 8. Check: 7 × 8 = 56. ✓ Always verify your bar model with multiplication.

What Is a Fact Family?
A fact family is a set of related multiplication and division facts using the same 3 numbers. Example: 3, 8, 24 → 3×8=24, 8×3=24, 24÷3=8, 24÷8=3. All four equations share the same three numbers.
🚩 mark
LEARNLN_02concrete
Square Number Families
When both factors are the same, the fact family has only 2 unique facts. Example: 7, 7, 49 → 7×7=49 and 49÷7=7. (7×7 and 7×7 are the same, so no flipping is needed.)
🚩 mark
LEARNLN_03pictorial
Writing Related Facts Step by Step
To write a fact family: (1) Pick 3 numbers where two multiply to give the third. (2) Write both × orders: a×b=c and b×a=c. (3) Write both ÷ facts: c÷a=b and c÷b=a. Result: 4 facts total.
🚩 mark
LEARNLN_04pictorial
Fact Family Triangle
Draw a triangle with the product at the top and the two factors at the bottom corners. Arrows from top show the two ÷ facts; arrows going up show the two × facts. Try: 5, 8, 40 → 5×8=40, 8×5=40, 40÷5=8, 40÷8=5.
🚩 mark
LEARNLN_05abstract
The Fact Family Rule
Given any two factors a and b with product c: the family is {a×b=c, b×a=c, c÷a=b, c÷b=a}. If a=b (square), the family shrinks to 2: {a×a=c, c÷a=a}. Knowing one fact means you know the whole family!

Divide by 1

Divide by Itself
Any number divided by itself equals 1. Think: if you have 8 items and split them into 8 groups, each group gets exactly 1. 8 ÷ 8 = 1. This works for every number (except 0).

Zero in Division
0 divided by any number is 0. (0 ÷ 5 = 0 — zero items shared among groups gives zero each.) But you can NEVER divide by 0. There's no answer for 7 ÷ 0.

The Four Division Rules
Rule 1: n ÷ 1 = n (any number ÷ 1 stays the same). Rule 2: n ÷ n = 1 (any number ÷ itself = 1). Rule 3: 0 ÷ n = 0 (zero divided by anything = 0). Rule 4: n ÷ 0 = undefined (you CANNOT divide by 0).
