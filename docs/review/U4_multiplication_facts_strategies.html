Making a Table
A table organizes information. Put the input in one column and the output in another. Look for a rule — like ×3 or ×7 — that connects input to output.
🚩 mark
LEARNLN_02concrete난이도 0
Finding the Rule
If 1 → 4, 2 → 8, 3 → 12, the rule connecting input and output is ×4. Check: 1×4=4 ✓, 2×4=8 ✓, 3×4=12 ✓.
🚩 mark

Extending the Table
Once you know the rule, you can predict any row. Rule: ×5. What is the output for input 9? Answer: 9 × 5 = 45.
🚩 mark
LEARNLN_04pictorial난이도 0
Multi-Step Problems
Some problems need two steps: multiply first, then add or subtract. Example: 4 boxes of 6 cookies, then eat 5. Step 1: 4 × 6 = 24. Step 2: 24 − 5 = 19 cookies left.

When you read a word problem: (1) find the two numbers being grouped, (2) decide if you multiply to find the total, (3) watch for extra steps like + or − at the end. Underline the key words!

The Distributive Property
You can break apart one factor into two smaller numbers, multiply each separately, then add. Example: 5 × 7 = 5 × (5 + 2) = (5 × 5) + (5 × 2) = 25 + 10 = 35.

Choosing How to Break Apart
Break the bigger factor into numbers you know well. If you know your 5s facts, break 7 into 5 + 2. If you know your doubles, break 8 into 4 + 4. There's no single right way — pick easy pieces.
